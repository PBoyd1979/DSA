Medic! - who will use it?

Having previously identifed and validated the need for the Medic! app, we were able to interview a number of interested parties from our core demographic, which led us to be able to define a persona for our product.

Personas

We have been able to clearly identify our audiences based on our research and analysis from conducted interviews, an online questionnaire, and a product landing page we had instated.
With our semi-structured interview questions, we identified users' needs, their age group, and the personalities of those that will be using our product.

Our target audience is people between 18-35 who are travelling on a budget, this would include people like backpackers & students.

Below is our main Persona:

The Traveller – from our data gathering, it was very clear to us that this persona could be active user of our product. A student or backpacker who loves to travel around the world, most times to country that does not speak their native language. For this user, having our product would be very convenient and could easily become a necessity for them before analysis travel.

Name Marie-Élise Martin
Age 29
Location Bordeaux, France
Occupation Primary school teacher
Education BA(Hons) English Language
Dream job Environmental campaigner

"Last year I visited over 12 countries in 3 different continents. I love to engage and meet new people"

Marie is a consistent traveller. One of those active people with a consistent smile on their face.

She lives with her boyfriend in Bordeaux, where she teaches an amazing group of kids, something she is very passionate about.

Being sick isn’t something we as humans are able to predict, especially when we are usually healthy, but Maria loves to know she can have it under control especially when she is out travelling around the world.

The great wall of China, checking out the wildlife in south Africa, and having a coffee with friends somewhere in London, are among the many things Maria hopes to do within the next year, therefore she needs to be comfortable in knowing she has the required resources on her mobile device to diagnose herself.
